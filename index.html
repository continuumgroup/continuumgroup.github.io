Continuum is a National Day of Civic Hacking project, dedicated to helping alleviate the problems of homelessness.

Here's what we've been up to:

Our Story

On June 1, 2013, T-REx in downtown St. Louis, MO hosted a National Day of Civic Hacking and Random Hacks of Kindness event. The goal of this event was to promote the use of technology to enhance our lives as citizens. During that time, 7 of us came together to build the prototype of Continuum.

1,370,000 of the homeless population are children under the age of 18.
(International Journal of Psychosocial Research, 2008)

Near the start of the event, we formed a team to build something to help homeless people in St. Louis. As we tried to flesh out what our concept would look like in real life, in walked James Ware. 13 years ago, James was homeless. He challenged us to look beyond our conceptions of homelessness and the shelter system and meet the real and pressing needs of people whose daily life is on the streets.

With James' knowledge, we set out to build a hotline to pair people on the street with beds in shelters. This system will remove the guesswork of finding a safe place to stay the night. Additionally, it will allow shelters to redirect people to an appropriate shelter instead of turning them away, should they run out of room. All this is accomplished in roughly five minutes. This is an improvement on the current coordination system, on which we spent 20 minutes on hold during a test.

30 to 45% of homeless people have and use cell phones to communicate with family and employers.
(Washington Post, 2009)

We plan on launching in the first quarter of 2014, targeting shelters in the St. Louis metro area. We are a team of developers, designers, and businesspeople, all convinced that we can ease the problem of homelessness in our community.
